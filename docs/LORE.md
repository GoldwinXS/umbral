# UMBRAL — Lore Bible v2

*The world and story that inform how the game is built. v2 (Fable): expanded from
the 3-level arc to the shipped 8-site campaign; adds the geography of Lanternspire
and a named relic for every mission. Supersedes v1. `docs/PLACES.md` owns the
per-site build law; this document owns WHY the sites exist and what Hush's journey
through them means. Where the two disagree, PLACES.md wins on geometry, LORE.md
wins on names and story.*

## The World

Before the citadel, there was the Old Dark — not an absence but a substance, a slow
black tide that filled the valleys of the world. An order of light-priests called
the **Candent Vigil** learned that darkness could be caught, caged, and *bled*:
rendered down, it burns. Every lamp in their obsidian citadel, **Lanternspire**,
runs on it. Their faith is simple and monstrous — light is order, dark is fuel, and
the Vigil's holy work is to keep the fires fed.

The lamps themselves are the Vigil's soldiers. The **Vespers** are floating
lantern-sentinels, monks who gave their eyes to the flame; they are blind to shadow
and see only what their own lamplight touches. Their cones of light are literally
their sight. The **Snuffed** are the order's penitents — Vespers who let their
flame die and now serve lightless, hunting by ear alone in the places where no
flame is permitted. Above them all hangs the **Pharos**, the Great Eye — the
Vigil's first and holiest engine, a vast unblinking lens whose sweeping beam is
both worship and alarm.

You are **Hush**. When the Vigil bled the Old Dark in their undercroft, one clot of
it survived the rendering — small, mute, and hollow, with two violet eyes that turn
red when the hunger takes over. Hush does not know what it is. It only knows that
something was taken from it, that the taking is *ongoing*, and that everything it
swallows makes it feel slightly more like itself. Every lamp in Lanternspire is
burning a piece of Hush's body. Its hunger is not appetite. It is homecoming.

## The City — where each mission stands

Lanternspire is a terraced citadel built on the drained bed of the valley the Old
Dark once filled: a mountain of light standing in the print of the thing it killed.
The campaign is one continuous journey — **up the terraces to the crown, then down
under it** — and every mission select, briefing, or map should read as a rung on
that ladder:

| # | Site | Where it stands | What it is to the city |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | **The Ashway** | the outermost terrace, against the wall | the burned lamplighters' street where snuffed and broken lamps are dumped — the city's ash-heap, and Hush's cradle |
| II | **The Dousing Yards** | the service ring above the wall | where street-lanterns come to be scoured, refuelled, and re-lit — the trade of light, seen from below |
| III | **The Fleshers' Row** | the market terraces | the night-market that feeds the trade: tallow, fat, and marrow bound for the vats — light's bloody kitchen |
| IV | **Brightward Gate** | the garrison gate between low city and high | the one gate to the upper terraces, and the parade square where the Vigil shows the dark is beaten |
| V | **The Lanternways** | the canal district of the high city | mirror-water canals that double every lamp — the Vigil's vanity, a city admiring its own light |
| VI | **The Great Chandlery** | the manufactory quarter under the crown | the works where all of it becomes candles — the industrial heart, boiling under open sky |
| VII | **The Vigil Spire** | the crown of the citadel | the order's own house: cells, scriptorium, and the roost of the Pharos |
| VIII | **The Mirror Vault** | the undercroft BENEATH the Spire | the reliquary over the sealed bleeding-cellar where Hush was born — the journey ends where it began, one terrace deeper than the ash |

The shape of the campaign: ash → service → meat → gate → water → fire → eye →
grave. Missions I–III climb through the city's servants, IV forces its one door,
V–VII walk its pride, and VIII goes under it. The Vault sits directly below the
Spire; the last two missions are one vertical line — the axis of the Vigil's whole
faith, and Hush climbs it both ways.

## The Relics

Each site guards one relic: a piece of concentrated first-light the Vigil venerates
— which is to say, a rendered piece of Hush. Swallowing each one returns a memory.
The relic is always the lit, venerated, deepest thing in its site (PLACES.md law),
and its name should appear wherever the mission is named (menu, briefing, HUD).

| # | Relic | What the Vigil says it is | What it actually is |
|---|---|---|---|
| I | the **Wickstone** | the ember of the first wick ever lit | the first bite taken from the tide |
| II | the **Everwick** | the wick no water can drown, throned in the yards' Great Lantern | proof they could make the theft permanent |
| III | the **Marrowlight** | a lamp burning the marrow-tallow of the first beast fattened on the dark | the first time they fed the dark to something else |
| IV | the **Noonstaff** | the scepter paraded to prove the dark is beaten | a trophy cut from the tide's deepest hour |
| V | the **Drowned Lantern** | the one lamp that burns beneath the canal's surface | a piece of the tide imprisoned in its own element |
| VI | the **Light-Heart** | the master-gem every candle is lit "in the image of" | the mould they render against — the shape of a wound |
| VII | the **Iris** | the small first lens from which the Pharos was ground | the eye they built to watch for the dark coming home |
| VIII | the **First Ember** | the oldest light-heart, the founding flame | the largest surviving piece of what Hush used to be |

## The Story — eight beats

- **I — Waking (The Ashway).** Hush wakes in the ash-pits among the snuffed lamps —
  the one place the Vigil throws its dark-fed fires to die. Tiny, starving, pulled
  upward. It learns to move, to be silent, to eat. The Wickstone gives it a first
  flash: a vast black tide, and men with hooks of light.
- **II — The Trade (The Dousing Yards).** Following the pull, Hush crosses the
  yards where light is serviced like a herd is watered. First understanding: the
  lamps are not many things. They are one thing, portioned. The Everwick tastes
  *familiar*.
- **III — The Kitchen (The Fleshers' Row).** The market that feeds the vats. Hush
  learns what rendering is by watching the trade in fat and marrow — and does some
  rendering of its own (this is the mission that teaches DEVOUR). The Marrowlight
  shows it: they fed the dark to beasts, and the beasts to the fires.
- **IV — The Theft (Brightward Gate).** The one gate to the high city, and the
  Noonstaff paraded behind it. Hush steals the proof that the dark is beaten, and
  understands the flash wasn't a dream. The lamps aren't just lamps. They're
  wounds. Its wounds.
- **V — The Mirror (The Lanternways).** The high city admires itself in its canals.
  In the doubled light Hush first SEES itself at size — a black absence moving
  through a city of reflections. The Drowned Lantern remembers being water: the
  tide had a surface once, and stars.
- **VI — The Works (The Great Chandlery).** Under the noon sun — the one lamp the
  Vigil never had to steal — Hush walks the floor where the theft is industrialized.
  The Light-Heart is the worst memory yet: not pain — *shape*. This is the exact
  form they cut from it, over and over.
- **VII — The Eye (The Vigil Spire).** Up the processional stair through the
  order's own house, under the Pharos' roost. The Iris shows the truth of the Eye:
  it was never watching for thieves. It was built on the founding night, and it has
  only ever watched for one thing — the dark coming back for its body.
- **VIII — The Turn (The Mirror Vault).** Beneath the Spire, past the scrying pools
  and the empty case where the Noonstaff stood, above the sealed cellar where Hush
  was born a leftover. Swallowing the First Ember, every lamp in Lanternspire
  gutters at once — the fuel is leaving. Hush was never the thief. The Vigil was.
  The final escape runs as a beacon through a dying citadel, the Pharos screaming,
  the Vespers going out one by one — not because Hush attacks them, but because
  what they burn is going home. Last image: a dark city, silent, and two violet
  eyes moving away — sated, whole, and enormous.

## Tone & Motifs

Hushed, patient, faintly sorrowful — a predator's ghost story where the monster is
the victim. Never gleeful; the red-eyed feed should feel like grief satisfied.

Recurring images: light as extraction (hooks, racks, wicks, bleeding); sound as
ripples on black water; a snuffed lamp releasing a thread of smoke that bends
toward Hush like iron to a magnet; the Vigil's litany carved everywhere — **"KEEP
THE FIRES FED"** — which reads as piety early and as horror by beat VIII.

On-screen text: two voices only. Hush's half-memory fragments ("You remember being
larger.") and found Vigil liturgy for contrast. Prompts terse; world-building lives
IN the world (PLACES.md tableaux), not in text.

**Palette law:** amber / orange / red is the Vigil's own light; **violet belongs
to the Old Dark** — Hush, and the imprisoned residue of it the Vigil keeps
(drowned lanterns, dreg-lamps, crypt-lamps burn violet because of WHAT they burn).
The Vigil's own flames are never violet. When Hush's eyes go red, it is briefly
speaking the enemy's colour — that's the point.

## The Reliquary cellar inscriptions (read in sequence, north→south)

1. Before the citadel rose, this pit already held the dark.
2. The first Vespers were not lanterns, but chains.
3. Shadow does not burn. So they learned to bleed it instead.
4. Each lamp above was lit on a stolen dusk.
5. The vaults grew bright while the cellar grew silent.
6. One night the chains sang, and no one answered.
7. They sealed the stair and called the matter finished.
8. It was never finished. It was only sleeping.
