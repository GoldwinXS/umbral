# UMBRAL — Lore Bible v1

*The world and story that inform how the game is built. Drafted by the Fable model; the cellar inscriptions by a companion writer. Palette law and naming here are canon for on-screen text.*

## The World

Before the citadel, there was the Old Dark — not an absence but a substance, a slow black tide that filled the valleys of the world. An order of light-priests called the **Candent Vigil** learned that darkness could be caught, caged, and *bled*: rendered down, it burns. Every lamp in their obsidian citadel, **Lanternspire**, runs on it. Their faith is simple and monstrous — light is order, dark is fuel, and the Vigil's holy work is to keep the fires fed.

The lamps themselves are the Vigil's soldiers. The **Vespers** are floating lantern-sentinels, monks who gave their eyes to the flame; they are blind to shadow and see only what their own lamplight touches. Their cones of light are literally their sight. Above them all hangs the **Pharos**, the Great Eye — the Vigil's first and holiest engine, a vast unblinking lens whose sweeping beam is both worship and alarm.

You are **Hush**. When the Vigil bled the Old Dark in their undercroft, one clot of it survived the rendering — small, mute, and hollow, with two violet eyes that turn red when the hunger takes over. Hush does not know what it is. It only knows that something was taken from it, that the taking is *ongoing*, and that everything it swallows makes it feel slightly more like itself. Every lamp in Lanternspire is burning a piece of Hush's body. Its hunger is not appetite. It is homecoming.

## The Story

- **Beat 1 — Waking (The Ashway).** Hush wakes in the ash-pits where the Vigil dumps snuffed and broken lamps. Tiny, starving, pulled upward. It learns to move, to be silent, to eat — and swallowing its first relic, the **Wickstone**, gives it a first flash of memory: a vast black tide, and men with hooks of light.
- **Beat 2 — The Theft (Brightward).** Following the pull, Hush infiltrates the lamp-lit streets and steals the **Noonstaff**, the scepter the Vigil parades to prove the dark is beaten. Swallowing it, Hush grows — and understands the flash wasn't a dream. The lamps aren't just lamps. They're wounds. Its wounds.
- **Beat 3 — The Vault (The Reliquary).** Hush descends into the Vigil's inner vault, and beneath it, the cellar: the bleeding racks where the Old Dark was rendered into fuel. This is where Hush was born — as a *leftover*. Above burns the **First Ember**, the oldest light-heart, the largest surviving piece of what Hush used to be.
- **Beat 4 — The Turn.** Swallowing the First Ember, every lamp in Lanternspire gutters at once — the fuel is leaving. Hush was never the thief. The Vigil was. The final escape runs as a beacon through a dying citadel, the Pharos screaming, the Vespers going out one by one — not because Hush attacks them, but because what they burn is going home. Last image: a dark city, silent, and two violet eyes moving away — sated, whole, and enormous.

## Tone & Motifs

Hushed, patient, faintly sorrowful — a predator's ghost story where the monster is the victim. Never gleeful; the red-eyed feed should feel like grief satisfied.

Recurring images: light as extraction (hooks, racks, wicks, bleeding); sound as ripples on black water; a snuffed lamp releasing a thread of smoke that bends toward Hush like iron to a magnet; the Vigil's litany carved everywhere — **"KEEP THE FIRES FED"** — which reads as piety early and as horror by Beat 3.

On-screen text: two voices only. Hush's half-memory fragments ("You remember being larger.") and found Vigil liturgy for contrast.

**Palette law:** amber / orange / red belongs to the Vigil; **violet belongs only to Hush**. When Hush's eyes go red, it is briefly speaking the enemy's colour — that's the point.

## The Reliquary cellar inscriptions (read in sequence, north→south)

1. Before the citadel rose, this pit already held the dark.
2. The first Vespers were not lanterns, but chains.
3. Shadow does not burn. So they learned to bleed it instead.
4. Each lamp above was lit on a stolen dusk.
5. The vaults grew bright while the cellar grew silent.
6. One night the chains sang, and no one answered.
7. They sealed the stair and called the matter finished.
8. It was never finished. It was only sleeping.
